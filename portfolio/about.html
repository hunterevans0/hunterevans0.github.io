About

I grew up with technology. I had access to a computer my entire life.

  • I began programming in Scratch around 9 years old.
  • I got my first tablet at age 10.
  • I built a computer when I was 11.
  • By the time I was 13, I had a smart phone.
  • At age 14 I took an Intro to Computer Systems class in high school.

I lost interest of all things computer related after my freshman year of high school. Between ages 14 and 20, I studied a broad range of topics, specifically agriculture, psychology, sociology, and philosophy. I only briefly studied each of these topics. The next time I seriously looked at programming was at age 20.

I began working on my Associate Degree in Software Development at this time. I found myself immersed in the logic and capabilities of programming.
The courses I took the first year focused on client-side technologies, while the second year focused on server-side languages.

I will spend 2 years developing my problem-solving skills at The College of Western Idaho.
